Attraction of VR

Virtual reality, also known as VR, is something that isn’t real, but it makes people feel like that is real. In this way, a lot of electronics and social media can also be referred to as VR. However, strictly speaking, VR provides a sense of immersion– stereoscopic displays for our vision, haptic devices for our touch or body feeling, and spatial sound for our hearing. In a nutshell, virtual reality entails conversing our sense with equipment and immersing ourselves in a dynamic virtual world.
